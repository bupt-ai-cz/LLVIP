Abstract

It is a very challenging task to detect pedestrians in visible images under very low-light conditions due to the loss of effective target areas. Visible and infrared fusion can generate a single complementary image that has both rich detail information and effective target areas. In this paper, we present VIP-LF, a visible-infrared paired pedestrian dataset for low-light image fusion. This dataset contains 21664 images, or 10832 pairs, most of which were taken at very dark scenes. All of the images are strictly aligned in time and space. We evaluate the performance of several image fusion algorithms on the dataset, demonstrating the complementary effect of fusion on image information, and finding the deficiency of existing fusion algorithms. We also benchmark the pedestrian detection on our dataset, and the results of the experiments show that there is still much room for improvement in the detection of pedestrians in very low-light conditions. We believe the VIP-LF dataset will contribute to the community of computer vision by promoting image fusion and pedestrian detection in very low-light applications. In addition, the dataset can also be used to study multi-modal image registration and image style conversion. The dataset is being released and expanded.
